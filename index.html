Research

My research interests lie in self-supervised learning, representation learning, and gaze behavior. I am currently developing more robust self-supervised learning models inspired by infants’ visual behaviors. I am also interested in multimodal infant data, including but not limited to audio, EEG, and ECG signals.

Simulated Cortical Magnification Supports Self-Supervised Object Learning
Zhengyang Yu, Aubret Arthur, Chen Yu, Jochen Triesch
ICDL, 2025
arxiv

We trained self-supervised learning models and found that cortical magnification supports self-supervised object learning.

Toddlers' Active Gaze Behavior Supports Self-Supervised Object Learning
Zhengyang Yu, Aubret Arthur, Marcel C. Raabe, Jane Yang, Chen Yu, Jochen Triesch
PNAS (under review)
arxiv / code

We constructed a series of egocentric toddler gaze datasets and demonstrated, through representation learning, that toddlers' gaze behavior supports self-supervised object learning.
